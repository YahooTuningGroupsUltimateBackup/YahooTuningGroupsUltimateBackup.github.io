I don't know 19 ET music from dutch composers. In choral music, Badings had
write (in 31-tet) :
Contrasten (Hendrik de Vries), cinq lieder pour choeur a cappella, 1952.

You could also look at :

Hans Kox (born in 1930) : Vues des anges, pour baryton (Poem R.M. Rilke), 1959
(in 31-tet)

Ton de Leeuw (born in 1926) : Lamento Pacis, 1969
1- Hommage to Geualdo di Venosa
2- Hommage to Motokiyo Zeami
3- Hommage to Johannes Ockeghem
Car nos vignes sont en fleur, pour choeur a cappella, 1981

Jan van Dijk (born in 1918) had write in 31-tet :
Vierstemmige intonatie-oefingen, pour choeur mixte a cappella, 1949
Deuntje, pour deux choeurs mixtes a cappella, 1952.
Hymne, pour choeur mixte a cappella, 1952.

IVAN WYSCHNEGRADSKY himself, write his name "Wyschnegradsky" without any
accent. International russian transcription write it "Vyshnegradsky".
The Ivan Wyschnegradsky Association follows the composer decision.
That's why I am writing "Wyschnegradsky".

Opus 9 (with instrumental and orchestral works)
is available on a CD from Ensemble 2E2M, direction Paul Mefano.
