>Roland's PMA-5 has been out for more than a year. It supports Roland"s GS
>specification, an extension of General MIDI. My understanding is that all
>Roland GS instruments have one octave based microtuning per part, or MIDI
>channel, but that they may only be accessible through MIDI System Exclusive
>codes. If the sequencer in the PMA-5 allows the user to define and embed
>Sysex, then the unit would be capable of self-contained microtonal music,
>otherwise an external sequencer would be needed to generate the microtonal
>parts. Worst case, it ought to be possible to generate the Sysex microtuning
>file on your computer, record it on the PMA-5, then use that setup file as
>the basis for any compositions.
>
>The PMA-5 is listed at my Microtonal Synthesis web site, located at:

I am not sure that this question, what microtonal instruments you own
and play, was directed at me. But in the spirit if the reckless
pursuit of truth....

My main microtonal instrument is an SGI Indigo R3000, aging but still
in fine voice. The secondary instruments are a PC/Pentium166
(recently upgraded from a 60MHz), and a new PentiumII/266, which will
be upgraded to Linux as soon as the 166 has settled down. I also have
