> a FEELING in Partch's music that is extremely repellent to me, and
> this has been so since the first time I ever heard him, LONG before I
> knew anything about microtonality or his 43/oct tuning. And, this is
> VERY subjective...I just don't like listening to his stuff because of
> the "vibe" inherent in his music...

I had to work out my own feelings on this subject, and came to some sort
of resolution. HP's moods came from much deeper than the harmonies, but
must have had some sort of grave extramusical bitterness involved
(Extrabitter Music? Bitter Extramusic?). Actually, the person who
helped me deal with this the best was Dean Drummond, who hasn't been
given much credit around here for being the closest to HP's music, be it
on HC, string quartet or zoomoozophone. He pointed out that the
crassness is a built-in factor, and is not meant to be attractive. While
Harry's music is satirical to the LCD (see: "Exercises in Counterpoint
Tried in a Court of Ancient Ritual", or the "Do Not Loiter" section of
_Dreamer_), is must not be expected to lift the spirit like "Don't Worry,
Be Happy". None of it does, with the possible exception of the Lewis
Carroll songs. After abandoning the music for a while, I have come back
to it full-force, with a love and respect for its similarity to "Four
Saints In Three Acts"--unsubtle satire with tongue firmly in cheek. My,
don't I take myself too seriously sometimes.

Yours,
Adam
