Great! You've got it!
It was originally written in 12edo. It has form ababab..., a is sequence of dim chords: Cdim, C#dim, D#dim, Edim, etc., b is based on Cmi6 chord.
The asymmetry of a progression led me to shift every second chord quarter tone higher to place it in between the other chords. Two dim chords makes 8edo. In part b only the fifth from Cmi6 is 1/4tone risen. Cello is live with intonation correction using Elastic audio in Samplitude.
Live guitar was recorded in two takes, using ordinary guitar. Original 12edo dim sequence was recorded in one take and the 8edo Cmi6 with some retuned strings in the second. Every second chord from take 1 was shifted using Samplitude
Keyboard - organ (Korg M3r) and clavinet-like sound (Yamaha VL-70m) were played live on retuned synths.
Drums and bass (Roland SC-55) were step-sequenced.
Voice was recorded independently, without any relation to music. It was the slowed down many times (8 or so) with no pitch manipulation.
Thats all.

Thanks to all for the positive response.

And one more off-topic theme: what about lossless compression. I think mp3 or ogg are good as preview or for mobile players, but I prefer lossless formats (FLAC) for archiving. It gives me the possibility to recompress or decompress it in the future without loss of quality. But it is probably theme for another group. Sorry.

Milan
