I can only translate some titles as I'm not from Norway (I'm from Sweden though, so the languages are pretty close).

Som symra rein og blaa - Like the summer, clean and blue

Balladetone = Ballad tone
Rullermann: ruller is a kind of folk dance with two beats, mann = man
Nykkjen = The water sprite
Villfar Og Sylvklaar: Seems to be the name of a nature mythical ballad, but I can't make out the words. Might be something like two person's names, "Lost and of-course/self-apparent/obvious"
Bryllaupsmarsj = wedding march
Grisilla: ?
Habbor Og Signe: Seems to be the names of two people.
Den dag kjem aldri: That day never came
Kivlemoeyane: ?
Christus, Der Alting I Lave: ?Christ, where everything abounds?
Apostlerne Sat I Jerusalem: The apostles sat in Jerusalem
Den Foersta Sommerfugl: The first summer bird
Draumkvedet: The dream song
Tinnemannen: The tin man
Fyrispil: ? fire stick ? fire game ?
Vaarbuvatn: Vaar means spring, Buvatn seems to be a lake in Norway. doesn't make much sense to me.
Sigri: Name of a person
Guro Rid til Ottesang: Guro Ride(imperative) to Ottesang
Spor 19: Track 19

Here's a site I found where you can buy a CD.

http://www.grappa.musiconline.no/shop/displayAlbum.asp?id=24480&cid=4&sid=22
