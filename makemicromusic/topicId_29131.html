It's more than an exercise, but the mood is jittery, so I called it "My Doctor Says Caffeine".
(Based on some old advertisement for de-caffeinated coffee I used to find amusing.)

The tuning is a 36-note JI scale adjusted to 171edo. (171edo has no personality that I'm aware of.)

I'm not sure whether the contradictions -- or tensions -- in what I'm trying to do can generate energy or whether they're self-defeating. That ambivalence may come across in the piece, which seems fitfully assertive and despairing, to me.

Not bad, not good. 4 1/2 minutes.

I may do a few more pieces using some of this material, not sure.

Thanks for listen and comments.

I'll remember to include an Mp3 copy.

Over my not-terribly-fast Internet connection, on an old computer, the .aif format plays ok, so I had stopped including an Mp3 copy, but I'll resume doing that.

Yeah, despite having some overtone series chords with 13th partials, it uses a long-term 12-tone structure and foreground familiar scales mostly.

I'm still fascinated with trying to get self-similar machinery to produce different effects, so playing around with that will be a large part of what I'm trying to do.

I hope that the different things I'm trying to do will eventually come together -- either in some mathematical or organic unity, or as some kind of music that plays different elements off each other *without* unifying them.
