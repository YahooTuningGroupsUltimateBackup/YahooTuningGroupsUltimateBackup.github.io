16-TET has a nice pelog-like scale with five small steps and two large
steps. There isn't a single way of mapping this scale to note names that's
clearly better than any other way, but here's one possible way:

C=0 D=3 E=5 F=7 G=9 A=12 B=14 C=16

Note that most of the major and minor thirds are spelled the same as in
traditional notation, except that B-D is a major third. The notes in
between can be notated with sharps and flats.

C C# Db D Eb E Fb F F# G G# Ab A Bb B Cb C

And you can easily add the cents notation to these written notes as
necessary:

C+0 C#-25 Db+50 D+25 Eb+0 E-25 Fb+50 F+25 F#+0 G-25 G#-50 Ab+25 A+0 Bb-25
B-50 Cb+25 C+0

Another way of mapping the scale is like this:

C=0 D=2 E=4 F=7 G=9 A=11 B=13 C=16

This gives you minor thirds on C-E, F-A, and G-B (the traditional major
thirds) and major thirds on the other four (the traditional minor thirds):
the exact opposite of standard notation.

Markus,

If you have a Windows or Linux pc, you can download
Scala, http://www.xs4all.nl/~huygensf/scala
Then if you select "Options" in the Edit menu and
press the "Select" button in the "Note naming
options" area, you will find three different notation
systems for 16-tET, from Easley Blackwood,
Herman Miller and Erv Wilson, E16, M16 and W16
respectively.
