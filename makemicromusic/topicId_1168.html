I especially like the bit about developing intrepedity through
polyrhythms:

"
Intrepidness, or resolute fearlessness, in Anlo-Ewe view, is an
extraordinary strength of mind. It raises the mind above the troubles,
disorders and emotions which the anticipation or sight of great perils
is calculated to excite. It is by this strength that ordinary people
become heroes, by maintaining themselves in a tranquil state of mind
and preserving the free use of their reason under most surprising and
terrible circumstances.
"
