I found this:

146-cET (= ~8.22-EDO) is an ET of very low cardinality which
gives pretty good approximations to 5 of the intervals in the
Gyaling scale (at 146, 292, 438, and 876 cents), one that's
rather farther away (730 cents), and one oddball pitch
that's not even close (584 cents).

Note that 17-EDO also gives a good approximation of 5 of the notes.

In case you really did need to know the answer:
cardinality simply refers to the number of distinct degrees in a scale.

When Jacky suggested finding a Gyaling-equivalent ET, he wrote:

> From: jacky_ligon <jacky_ligon@...>

> the octave.

IOW, he was guessing that an ET with rather high cardinality
would be required to represent the Gyaling scale. So I took
that as a challenge to find one of only a few notes.

Assuming that "8ve"-equivalence is OK, your Gyaling scale
gives an interval matrix (in cents) of:

| 139 280 450 493 707 884
----+------------------------------
884 | 455 596 766 809 1023 0
707 | 632 773 943 986 0 177
493 | 846 987 1157 0 214 391
450 | 889 1030 0 43 257 434
280 | 1059 0 170 213 427 604
139 | 0 141 311 354 568 745
0 | 139 280 450 493 707 884

where the number from the left column is subtracted from the
number in the top row, mod 1200.

Then using this more stringent criteria for finding an
ET equivalent, I took a look at 34-EDO and again it seems
to be the winner.
