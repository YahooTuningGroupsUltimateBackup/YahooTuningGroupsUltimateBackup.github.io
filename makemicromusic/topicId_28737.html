On Tue, Jul 3, 2012 at 7:50 PM, Mike Battaglia <battaglia01@...> wrote:
>
> Sure, go ahead and build bridges. I also have an interest in replacing
> everything that paid software can do with things that free software
> can do.

Also, for the record, I don't really want to get into the fight here
or whatever went down. Sucks that Jeff's seen so much abuse in his
time in the community, and the story about the guy who wanted to track
down and kill his family was truly disturbing. I agree that nobody
should have to deal with that.

But I do think it's a shame that LMSO isn't out there anymore, since
it was a tool many people seemed to depend on for making actual
microtonal music. For instance, if Jeff wants to retire from the
community and internet flamewars, he can always open source LMSO or
put it out as freeware, as many retiring developers do. But from his
post it doesn't seem like that's his intention, and rather that he
wants LMSO to just be gone, period. To be honest I don't like that,
since it makes it harder for everyone to make music and seems like
kind of a "fuck all you guys guess what no more LMSO" type thing. But,
you know, it's his right to do whatever he wants with his software.

So now I'm just curious to see what was lost, and what you can't do in
Scala. I think the main thing was the functionality that retunes Logic
softsynths, so I wonder how hard it'd be to code up some simple app to
do some of that in JUCE or whatever. I don't actually have LMSO so I'm
not sure what its features are. I was hoping to purchase it in the
coming months, once I get a bit of money saved up (lol recession), but
that's not an option anymore.

-Mike
