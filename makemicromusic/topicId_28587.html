Quarter comma meantone (31 fifths). As Paur Erlich pointed out elsewhere, it has been rather thought of in terms of a chain of fifths (in a more or less conscious fashion).
More tunings could be tried out and any suggestions are welcome (please let me know if you prefer to be cited or not)! Please note that all the 31 tones of 31tet or quarter comma meantone are needed (in 19tet some couples of tones collapse).

hstraub64>Were there some special counterpoint rules applied here?
No, there were none: just parallel fifths and octaves forbidden :)
(BTW, it could be interesting to discuss if some parallels intervals more should be forbidden once we have more overtone approximants...but please do not forbid parallel thirds...)
However, the canon is unusual in that leader is in E and follower in F and translation from one to another is rigid.
Also, there are some inversions.
Best wishes,
Claudi

Carl Lumma>[...] Are you sure you didn't write this in 12?
The short answer is yes, but the initial idea (three or four measures) was born on a (nonmicrotonal) organ quite a few years ago. The dissonances are a consequences of the internal structure of the canon and of the costraint of forbidden fifths and octaves.

Carl Lumma>I don't get the use of cut time here... wouldn't 4/4 be easier to read (all half-notes becoming quarter notes)?

Basically I did it for matter of taste: I am traditionalist...Yet I acknowledge that this kind of tradition was already dead before I was born :)
