Art and Life
05/21/2006

In the summer of 2004 my 10-year-old son started taking pictures with
my girlfriend's digital camera. Within a month he was producing a
prolific stream of captivating images that clearly indicated a talent
for experimentation and composition.

But just how do we evaluate the possible merits of a preteen's
artistic abilities anyway? Well, this is a question with many
possible answers of course, yet in the end it's one I personally
prefer to view in the same way that the great New England composer
Charles Ives once wrote:

"The future of music may not lie entirely with music itself, but
rather in the way it makes itself a part withÂ—in the way it
encourages and extends, rather than limits, the aspirations and
ideals of the peopleÂ—the finer things that humanity does and dreams
of."

Because more than anything it was my son's innocent and honest
enthusiasm for making these pictures that stood outÂ… and, in my
opinion, this is the great province of art; especially as it relates
to young people, because I honestly believe that a thing born and
perpetuated by the means of your own interest and drive is a thing no
teacher can properly teach and a thing profoundly at the root of
building a meaningful art and character.

So I will continue to encourage and promote my son's creative
pursuits, but only to the extent of his own genuine interest. Because
for me these photos represent nothing less than our ongoing
collaboration as father and son, and in that there is a truth that
transcends all talent and skill and makes art a truer part of life.
