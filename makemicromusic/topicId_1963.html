> Q,#1, what exactly is a sruti?

hah, that's a good one! ;-)

the short answer is that it's the basic interval measurement
in Indian music, there being 22 of them in an "octave"
(instead of the 12 in the usual western scale).

the consensus today is that these are n o t equal
sizes. most Indian theorists and scholars of their
work categorize intervals in Indian scales into 3 basic
step-sizes, which are thus 3 different-sized srutis,
approximately 22, 70, and 90 cents.

see my webpage "5-Limit Implications of Ancient Indian Tuning"
http://www.ixpres.com/interval/monzo/indian/indian.htm

and the related dialog, mostly between me and Paul Erlich,
"The Indian sruti system as a periodicity-block"
http://www.ixpres.com/interval/td/erlich/srutipblock.htm

Paul gives what is probably the most "correct" interpretation
of the rational logic behind the Indian sruti system.

there's been a lot of discussion of srutis over on the
main tuning list
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/tuning

here's a fuller answer that i gave one of those times:
