That can work both ways! :)

For me, I always think a piece "feeling short" when it's long tends to be a
plus--I felt that way about Kraig Grady's "Beyond the Windows, perhaps among
the podcorn"....it's longer than an hour, but I just doesn't feel like
it....you get lost in it. It ends up feeling like a subjective 1/2 hour to
me.

Then there's the feeling, which you probably are trying to articulate, that
something carries "weight" or has a high "density", so it feels longer b/c
it packs a lot into some few minutes....I just have to disagree about the
density of this particular youtube piece, and I too, love a good Steve
Reich, John Adams, Michael Gordon or Phillip Glass piece!
