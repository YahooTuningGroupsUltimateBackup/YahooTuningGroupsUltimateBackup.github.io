Hi all

Have a look at :

http://240edo.tripod.com/id3.html

3 new music is uploaded :

* In a private mail , Dr.john chalmers
<http://www.dismec.unibo.it/musichegreci/intersoc/usa/chalmers.htm> said
that Ptolemy used a string length of 120 units to define both individual
tetrachords and full octave scales.

So , i made a music named , shurist
<http://240edo.tripod.com/sitebuildercontent/sitebuilderfiles/shurist.mp
3> in persian dastgah-e-shur and in 120-EDL which goes to a new mode
with folkish taste :

0: 1/1 0.000 unison, perfect prime
1: 40/37 134.970
2: 8/7 231.174 septimal whole tone
3: 20/17 281.358 septendecimal augmented second
4: 4/3 498.045 perfect fourth
5: 3/2 701.955 perfect fifth
6: 60/37 836.925
7: 12/7 933.129 septimal major sixth
8: 120/67 1008.962
9: 2/1 1200.000 octave

* With samples of my voice in 7-ADO , i made a music
<http://240edo.tripod.com/sitebuildercontent/sitebuilderfiles/choir-of-7
ado.mp3> :

0: 1/1 0.000 unison, perfect prime
1: 8/7 231.174 septimal whole tone
2: 9/7 435.084 septimal major third, BP third
3: 10/7 617.488 Euler's tritone
4: 11/7 782.492 undecimal augmented fifth
5: 12/7 933.129 septimal major sixth
6: 13/7 1071.702 16/3-tone
7: 2/1 1200.000 octave

* A music
<http://240edo.tripod.com/sitebuildercontent/sitebuilderfiles/tar-tombak
.mp3> for tar(played by my freind , mr.naderzadeh) and tombak(played by
me)on some gushehs and dastgahs of persian music , in a new style of
improvisation which tombak, as a drum , played out of meters .

dastgahs are : homayun , shur , mahur , chargah , segah , nava and
rastpanjgah.

tuning of tar has a fourth of about 485 cent:
