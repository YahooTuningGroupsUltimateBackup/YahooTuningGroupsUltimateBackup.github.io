List,

In an earlier post, Gene Ward Smith mentioned the following:

>the fact is you can already produce sounds which are clearly musical using >electronic means, even if they tend to horrify Jon.

Somehow Gene has completely missed the fact that about 90% of my recent work involves electronic means, and in fact the last large-scale piece was entirely electronic, entirely virtual (with the possible exception, if one is entirely anal-retentive, of the the fact that I utilized some samples of some of my acoustic percussion). Far from being horrified by "electronic means" (if ever there was a vague description!), I've actively worked with three different developers in encouraging (and in one instance, actively working on the beta/debugging team) the implementation of microtuning capabilities. I'm pleased to see that one of the companies, LinPlug, has just announced a new hybrid FM VST synth that is the third instrument they have released that contains microtonal support through direct importing of .tun files.

I find an awful lot of things in this world "clearly musical", and a lot of them relate to how, and how well, those resources are used. I wouldn't want anyone on this list to get the impression wrongly (as I believe in the power of truth) from Gene.

Thanks for the mention, Gene, and the opportunity to clear this up.

Cheers,
Jon
