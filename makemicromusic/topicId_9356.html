> Okay I have a G4 400 mhz machine.

Besides my G5 dual 2Ghz, I also have a G4 400Mhz laptop and a 400 Mhz desktop, which I had been using earlier for Logic, and still continue to use.
The problem with the slower 400 Mhz G4's is that as soon as you attempt to use more than about six virtual instruments simultaneously, you run out of live processing power.
In consequence you either have to freeze some of the tracks (i.e. convert to audio) or bounce them, which is fine unless you wish to later make changes, in which case you have to unfreeze the tracks,
do your changes and refreeze them, which can take a few minutes to process, and interrupts your creative workflow.

or has a bridge to Logic, you should be able to use it; otherwise you may just have to record its audio output into Logic,
if you are running it on another platform (e.g. M$), and I assume that you would sync it via MIDI, using Logic as the master MIDI sequencer.

> ;-) I saw a drama about longitude Harrison. It was very interesting!

It was probably the Granada film called "Longitude" from the Dava Sobel book, directed by Charles Sturridge of "Brideshead Revisited" fame, with Jeremy Irons, and Michael Gambon playing Harrison
I worked on that production here in Ham, near London and with one of the Oxford University choirs in 1999.
The action jumps between Harrison's era (1700's) and the 1920/30's, as his clocks are restored.
(I took more money in lieu of credits;-)
The result was a very good film biography of Harrison, which showed the scientific politics and intrigues of 18th and 20th century Britain in an interesting form.
Twenty-first century Britain still has many similarities;-)
It is now available on DVD from the public libraries here in UK, so prolly also elsewhere too, well worth watching if you are interested in scientific, naval, and political history, or like a good historical drama.
More info on it is at:

http://www.harmonics.com/lucy/lsd/granada.html
