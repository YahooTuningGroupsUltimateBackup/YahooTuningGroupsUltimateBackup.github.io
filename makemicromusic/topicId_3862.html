Let's see, there's a little paperback book called 'Horns Strings and
Harmony' by Arthur Benade that should get you started on the
mathematical structures. Book called 'Physics and Music' by Sir
James Jeans (whose wife played the clavichord....) is also good for
basics. History of temperament, well, mostly unreadable, alas, but
there's Mark Lindley, 'Lutes, Viols and Temperament', and a quite
new little book by Stuart Issakoff called 'Temperament' which is
fairly readable but alas gets mountains of things wrong. Piano
tuning, best book I have is 'Piano Tuning, Servicing and Rebuilding'
by Arthur Reblitz. And there is a huge book on the history of
temperament in pianos, sort of, called 'Tuning', by Owen Jorgenson,
which is fascinating, but expensive, specialized and I fear quite
misleading in many ways.
