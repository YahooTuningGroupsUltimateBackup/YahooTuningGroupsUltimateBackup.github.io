The technique I have been using to generate scores involves a cost
function which is based on harmonic relationships. The program starts
with a random score which has a high cost, and then slowly sifts
through the set of possible scores - of course, only through a tiny
subset of all the possibilities - step by step reducing the cost. In
the end the lowest cost score is pretty much a monotone. So both the
highest and the lost cost are boring. The interesting area is in
between. A few days I posted one of the in between snapshots. Here is
another snapshot further along in the process, i.e. at a lower cost. A
lot more repetitive and closer harmonies - should be easier to listen
to, though perhaps less interesting. The earlier one was mighty
dissonant, probably too much so.

http://soundclick.com/share?songid=7370526
