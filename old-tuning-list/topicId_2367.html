8/31/1997 7:16:01 AM
>If the sruti
>scale is influenced by Pythagorean ideas, why no mention of
>frequency ratios?

The ancient Hindu texts mention 9- and 13-sruti intervals (perfect
fourths and perfect fifths) as the only "consonant" intervals, and never
deviated from octave equivalence. That's as Pythagorean as you need to
get (Pythagoras had no idea about "frequency ratios," either!). Hindu
theorists probably found that by tuning 21 consecutive "consonant"
intervals, they could generate the existing scales with virtually no
error (2 cents) and have a roughly consistent measure of size for scale
steps (major tone = 4, minor tone = 3, semitone = 2). I do have one
other model (in my forthcoming paper) for how 22 could have come to be
used to describe their scales, but it is a little more far-fetched.
