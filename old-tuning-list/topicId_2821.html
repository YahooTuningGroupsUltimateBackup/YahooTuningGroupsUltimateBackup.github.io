I believe I referred to "Szasz" in a post. I meant Dr. O. Szende, whose
Intervallic Hearing, its Nature and Pedagogy (1977) is quite useful,
even if evidently flawed by 12-tone equal habits of thought.

By the way, the Russian Garbuzov (1948), cited by Szende, p. 57, found a
melodic limen, excluding the two extreme values, of from 58-70 cents,
with a mean between 63.5 and 64 cents. I suppose I shall be accused of
utter 19-tone equal prejudice in citing this...
