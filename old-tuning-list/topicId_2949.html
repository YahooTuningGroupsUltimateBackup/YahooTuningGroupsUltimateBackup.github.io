Neil: The scale is the 14-tone stellated hexany or dekatesserany, Mandala,
or tetradekany invented by Erv Wilson. It is generated by taking any 2)4
hexany and adding 8 new tones to complete the generating tetrad or its
inversion at each facet. That is, if the generator is A.B.C.D, the extra
tones are A^2, B^2, C^2, D^2 and A.B.C/D, A.B.D/C, A.C.D/B and B.C.D/A.
The original hexany tones are A.B, A.C, A.D, B.C, B.D and C.D

When A = 1 as in 1.3.5.7, the math is simpler as 1^2 =1/1 and is a
convenient tonic. The tones thus are 1/1 21/20 15/14 35/32 9/8 5/4 21/16
35/24 3/2 49/32 25/16 105/64 7/4 15/8 2/1, but actually any of the 14 modes
can be used. The scale is intervallically symmetrical about 21/16 and 15/8.

I don't know which piece is on the tape Brian sent you, but Paul Rapoport
set a poem by Hildegard of Bingen to this scale and recorded it (See
MusicWorks for article and recording). The Sonic Arts players in San
Diego have based improvisations on this scale as well.

--John
