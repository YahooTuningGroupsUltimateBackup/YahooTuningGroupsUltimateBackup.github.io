I've been seeing this reference to Cage's comments on microtonalism being "just
another wing on the academy" for long enough to begin wondering precisely what
John might have meant. I wonder - do any of you have a source for this comment?
I'd like to take a look at the remark in its original context.

with regards,
Gregory

By the way, isn't the original quote about art resembling nature (like the quote
about the purpose of art being "to quiet the mind and render it susceptible to
divine influences") *also* a paraphrase of the Indian aesthetician Coomaraswamy?
One might also keep in mind that those epistemic traditions which see the
natural
world as physical manifestations of forms of thought or principles of action and
movement which reveal themselves to those to take the physical as "the stuff of
contemplation" might mean something very different when using using a
phrase like
"to resemble nature in her mode and method of operation" than you or I
might mean.

K'un/K'un, y'all*
Gregory
