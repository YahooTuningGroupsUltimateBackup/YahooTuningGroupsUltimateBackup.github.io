Giacinto Scelsi festival in New York (remaining events)
Scelsi in Chelsea
Frances-Marie Uitti performs 'The Trilogy' for solo cello. >Tuesday,
February 25, 8pm. @ The Kitchen (212)255-5793 Tickets: $12.50

Scelsi and Friends
Music by Giacinto Scelsi, Morton Feldman, and John Cage, introduced by
Heinz-Klaus Metzger, played by Aki Takahashi, Frances-Marie Uitti, and
others. >Thursday, February 27, 8pm. @ Guggenheim Museum (212)423-3587
Tickets: $15

Continuum
Co-directors Cheryl Seltzer and Joel Sachs present Scelsi's 'Okanagon,'
'Khoom', 'Kya', and other chamber works. >Saturday, March 1, 8pm. @ Merkin
Hall. (212)501-3330 Tickets: $12.50

For further info:

http://www.emf.org/calp_scelsi0297.html

Interpretations
David Tanenbaum & Paul Dresher
Sorrell Hays
David Tanenbaum & The Paul Dresher Ensemble
Works mostly for acoustic, electric, and electronically-enhanced guitar by
Toru Takemitsu, Bryan Johanson, Steve Reich, Paul Dresher, Jay Cloidt,
David Lang, Eve Beglarian, and Aaron Jay Kernis, played by David Tanenbaum,
The Paul Dresher Ensemble, and guest David Abel. >Thursday, March 6, 8pm. @
Merkin Hall. (212)501-3330 Tickets: $17.50

Sorrell Hays
As part of the 'Henry Cowell's Musical World' festival, Sorrel Hays pays
tribute to Cowell's legacy with a performance of his cluster piano music.
Music also by Sorrell Hays and Annea Lockwood, performed by Ms. Hays (piano
and keyboards), Thomas Buckner (baritone), Brian Charles (oboe), and
Marilyn Ries (electronics). >Thursday, March 13, 8pm. @ Merkin Hall.
(212)501-3330 Tickets: $10

For further information on these concerts and the Interpretations series:

http://www.emf.org/calp_interpretations.html

Other March events in New York
Henry Cowell's Musical Worlds
A centennial festival celebrating the life and music of the American
experimental composer, consists of symposia, lectures, concerts, and an
exhibition. >Wednesday, March 12 to Tuesday, March 25. @ various places
around town. Some events are free. The schedule is available at

http://www.emf.org/calp_cowell0297.html

Cloud Chamber
Bruce Odland and Sam Auinger's installation/performance will co-exist from
Wednesday, March 12 to Saturday, March 15 in cyberspace and at The Kitchen.
(212)255-5793 For info, email ores@pipeline.com or go to

http://www.aec.at/residence/cc/

March in Paris
Son-Mu 97
This concert series, presented annually by the INA-GRM, gets underway with
music by Francois Bayle, Luciano Berio, Daniel Teruggi, Pierre Henry,
Jean-Claude Risset, Denis Smalley, Jonty Harrison, Julio Viera, Leo Kupper,
and many others. >Tuesday, February 25, 6:30pm. Sunday, March 16 @ 6:30pm.
Other events through June. @ Maison de Radio France. All events are free.
See the schedule at

http://www.emf.org/calp_sonmu0197.html

60th Parallel
IRCAM presents the world premiere of this new opera by Philippe Manoury,
Michel Deutsch, and Pierre Strosser. The plot revolves around interactions
between passengers in an airport near the 60th parallel, with Manoury using
electronic processing and spatialization to enhance the drama of their
interactions. >Monday, March 10, 7:30pm. Thursday, March 13, 7:30pm.
Sunday, March 16, 5pm. Wednesday, March 19, 7:30pm. @ Theatre du Chatelet.
4233-0000 for information, 4028-2840 for reservations. Tickets: from 60 to
400 francs.

Other events in various locations
Multidisciplinary Music & Notation Conference
This conference is sponsored by Grame, a research and presenting
organization in Lyons (France). As the organizers point out, "A wide range
of artistic and scientific disciplines make use of notational systems ...
They reflect a state of knowledge as well as a vision of the world, but
they are also a decisive element in the evolution of their discipline."
>Friday, March 21 to Sunday, March 23. For further information, go to

http://www.grame.fr/RMPD/RMPD97/presentation_us.html

102nd AES Convention
The 102nd is in Munich. >Saturday, March 22 to Tuesday, March 25. For
further information, go to

http://www.aes.org/events/102/

About this calendar
The full version of The EMF Worldwide Calendar is on the web. You'll find a
comprehensive monthly list of events, a Don't Miss This Event page, special
reports, pointers to web sites, news from our sponsors, and more. You'll
also find instructions for submission of event information. Go to

http://www.emf.org/cal_frontdoor.html

This email version of the calendar is a digest version of the Don't Miss
This Events page. We're planning to publish this email version of The EMF
Worldwide Calendar for a few months on an experimental basis. Whether we
continue will depend upon whether or not you find this useful and whether
or not you'll participate. So please let us know what you think. If you
need information on submitting information, contact us at

calendar@emf.org

We maintain separate email lists for this calendar (sent around the 20th of
every month), montly updates (sent around the 5th of every month), and
occasional catalogs. If you would rather not receive this calendar, simply
send us a message saying "Don't send me the calendar" and we will take you
off the calendar list.
