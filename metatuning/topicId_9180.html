Here's a review I sent to the Merzbow list of the show at the Knitting
Factory on Tuesday. In case you're not familiar with Merzbow (real name
Masami Akita), he's the reigning high priest of noise electronic music. used
to be analog but switched to laptops about 5 yrs ago. Has released a couple
of hundred (sic) albums over 20 years. Theres even a 50cd boxset(called
"Merzbox") that, for $500 will be delivered to your domicile by courier. Has
become somewhat more beat oriented in recent years (although not at this
show) actually has quite alot of variety over his many albums.

I wuz at NYC show @ Knitting Factory on Tuesday. "Unbelievably loud" is
certainly one way to describe it. It was like sitting on the beach in
Louisiana as the hurricane was making landfall... or maybe it was more like
the World Trade Center coming down on your head. No Merzbeats at all: more
like Scumtron, just pure blowtorch noise all across the spectrum. Acoustic
pressure waves blasting your body just as much as your ears. I had a book
with me which I held over my sternum to try and protect my heart from the
low frequency rumblings- it felt like it could mess with or stop your
heartbeat - but of course the waves are in your whole body. People started
leaving after about 10 minutes (just a few here and there). Masami Akita
sitting up there looking like a white skull with sunglasses and long black
hair between two Mac laptops, expressionless, yet oddly serene. The club
audience standing motionless, I imagine each of us basically holding on for
dear life.

Yes, it certainly was unbelievably loud- easily the loudest "music" (is that
even the right word?) I've ever heard/felt. Kinda existential, actually, in
that it makes you "be there" 110%. You ain't daydreaming about what to have
for lunch the next day, thats for sure. Just breathing into the sound,
fighting/resisting is what is uncomfortable. I'll never forget it but I cant
say I will ever want to repeat it either- like if I survived a plane crash I
doubt I would want to be in another! Not for the squeamish. 99.99% of the
population would run screaming from the room within minutes.

When you listen on stereo or ipod you can adjust volume so that its just as
loud as you want, but not too loud, also you can pause or turn it off
easily. Its a whole different story in a club with massive speaker arrays
and Masami Akita controlling the volume (sounded like it was just turned up
to "11" the whole time) as the minutes tick by slowly, slowly as the
unstoppable avalanche engulfs you with threatened annihilation. It really
was quite amazing, but I must admit I felt kinda ill the next day like I had
been beat up or something. Bizarro.

Dante
