It's a very interesting problem. There are several, not
mutually exclusive explanations worth looking into...

() Cancer is accelerated by silent viral infections, which
are proliferating in our increasingly dense societies.

() Cancer diagnosis is improving.

() People are living longer, therefore cancer is increasingly
what's killing them.

() Stress contributes to cancer over time, and we're
increasingly stressed.

() Fresh vegetables and fruits help prevent cancer, and
we're eating less and less of them.

() Carcinogens are building up our environment.

Blaming "corporations" for any of this is silly. Several
of these things may be due to stupidity, but not to
"corporations".

By the way, my own personal leanings are, and have always
been, in the tribalist direction (whatever that means). At
any rate, the current style of American life hardly suits
me. But I don't blame "corporations", or any particular
economic/political model. I'm sure what I like about
"tribalism" could be conducted equally well with socialist
or capitalist structures.

If drugs and diagnostics could really beat it, the
"corportations" stand to get paid *less* than they currently
are. In fact, the willingness of western medicine to wipe
out diseases has been demonstrated. Name an ailment
wiped out by chiropractors or accupuncturists -- who also
handle billions of dollars a year in business.
