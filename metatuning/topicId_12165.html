At the very least, I'm extremely skeptical of anything the government
says about anyone, or for that matter, the media, when it comes to
anything pertaining to 9/11.

While we are on the subject, David Ray Griffen's latest book, "9/11
Contradictions - an Open Letter to the Congress and the Press" is
amazingly good. He writes for the idiots who don't realize that any
theory about 9/11 is by definition a 'conspiracy theory' (i.e. those
who say "I don't believe in 'conspiracies'" while believing that at
least 19 hijackers conspired to fly jets into US symbolic targets).

So in essence, he says "Ok, let's forget 'conspiracy' and just look at
the internal problems with the government story as represented by the
flawed 9-11 Commision Report, mainly the internal contradictions and
provably false statements, multiple versions of events and timelines,
etc." It's really an incisive and maddeningly frightening read--you
realize how bad it is in America today. Stalin light, but with more
streamlined and subtle 'psy-ops'.
