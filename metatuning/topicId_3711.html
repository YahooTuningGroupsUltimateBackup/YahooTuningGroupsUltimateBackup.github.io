The percentage of "Caesarian births", as they are now called,
is routinely 25% across the country. The 18% number at
Alta Bates (our hospital) is considered very low. It's been
steadily rising since the '70s, apparently. Fear of litigation,
throughput constraints in hospital wards, and an increased use
of pain-relief interventions are all possible explanations.
And a small but not insignificant contribution comes from
"planned Caesarian births", where the mother schedules a
c-section rather than waiting for labor... increasingly popular,
incredibly. I shudder to think of the consequences for a
generation born this way. Respiratory problems are a well-known
complication of c-sections, and important aspects of mother-baby
bonding are tied to "vaginal birth", as it is now called, and
these are only the most obvious considerations.

-Carl
