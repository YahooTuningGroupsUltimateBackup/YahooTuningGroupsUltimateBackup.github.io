Brother Mohajeri,

Although I do not appreciate the Khomeini regime in Iran or other so-called Shariah states around the world usurping power in the name of muslims everywhere, I nevertheless know that the Quran is the ultimate guideline for a muslim. The Sunnah of our prophet is valid to a certain degree as well. We are afterall commanded by Allah to fight in the name of our Creator to free the oppressed and persecuted. We are required to obeisance and the observance of the divine laws. We may not defer to the judgment of any faction but the muslim ummah. Yes, there is much controversy as to what this ummah is, and who represents it. The dichotomy between the clergy and innate Islamic democracy is disconcerting to say the least. Yet, we may not lead a life where we end up serving the moral requirements of a non-Islamic entity. Even if you live abroad, you would do well to resist the incursions of `ethics` propounded by infidel societies.

It is not possible to render Allah Almighty weak in the heavens or the earth. His Will works in such ways that we are not able to comprehend fully His intent. However, I do correlate earthquakes and tornados with divine retribution. For the believers, it is a trial, to let us see for ourselves whether we are hypocrites or true believers, whether we can endure hardships due to our faults or not. For the disbelievers it is a stern warning and a sign from their Lord. The one whose heart Allah favors to faith shall believe, the rebellious will reject and increase in their disbelief. It is all written in the Holy Quran, word for word.

Allah is Compassionate, Merciful. Yet, He is also Mighty, Stern in punishment. Allah is the embodiment of the the most virtuosic attributes. He is a God against Whom none can lift a finger without His leave.

Cordially,
Ozan
