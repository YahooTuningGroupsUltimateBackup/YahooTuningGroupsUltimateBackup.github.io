--- In tuning-math@yahoogroups.com, Kraig Grady <kraiggrady@a...> wrote:
> have you cracked the code on this one?

No, and it's a pretty strange-looking scale, with all those
schisma intervals in it. By far the most obvious way to temper it is
garibaldi, the 41&53 temperament. That boils the whole thing down to
Garibaldi[43], which is a hell of a big difference from a 100 note
scale. But with 43 notes out of 94, you could just go to the 94 and
you'd still have fewer notes than the 100 you started with.
