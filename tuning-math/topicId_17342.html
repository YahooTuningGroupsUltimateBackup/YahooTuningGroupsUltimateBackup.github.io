robert thomas martin wrote:
> Whenever 53tet is mentioned it should also be noted that 41tet is a > more simple solution. The difference between 5ths is minimal and 41tet > has to put up with 12 extra notes if 53tet is chosen. It is simply a > matter of economy.

34-ET is even simpler. There are always tradeoffs of complexity vs. accuracy. If you're dealing with 5-limit harmony, 53-ET has better thirds. 31-ET and 34-ET also have better thirds, but the fifths aren't as good as 41-ET. For 11-limit harmony, on the other hand, 41-ET looks better.

Whether 53 is much less convenient than 41 depends on what you're using it for. Both work well on a Bosanquet keyboard, and neither one works well with the 128-note limitation of MIDI. 72-ET is a useful tuning for many purposes even though it has many more notes than 53 or 41.
