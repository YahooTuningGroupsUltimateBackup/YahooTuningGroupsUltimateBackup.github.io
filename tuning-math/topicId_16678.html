is the center of symmetry of the black white keyboard, along with D.

we go to the 7-limit and consider also 49/48 instead of 16/15.
So now it is about the distinction between two kinds of chromatic
half-steps...

The pyramidal number function is

pyr(n) = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6

and

pyr(n)-1 = (n-1)(2n^2+5n+6).

This isn't quite as well adapted as squares or triangles for producing
interesting results, but for instance 385 is neither square nor
triangular, but is pyramidal, and 385/384 is an important 11-limit
comma.

I should have said Siegel's theorem. Neither theorem is about square or
triangular numbers, but elliptic curves with rational coefficients.
Mordell's theorem says that the (group theoretic) rank of such a curve
is finite, and Siegel's theorem says that there are only a finite
number of integer points on it. If we have, for example, the elliptic
curve

y^2 = x(x+1)(2x+1)/6

then by Siegel's theorem there are only a finite number of pairs of
integers (x, y) satisfying the equation.
