I'd proposed comma sequences as unique identifiers for linear
temperaments which displayed their family relationships, but there
were some problems with the definition. Another approach is to do what
I just did with equal temperaments; first use reduction to Hermite
normal form, giving a comma sequence whose first element is a 5-limit
comma. Then reduce the remaining commas with respect to this comma,
getting a list of, mostly, 5-limit bridge commas. Family similarity is
reflected in identity of the first n commas. Below is a list of comma
sequence identifiers for 11-limit linear temperaments.
