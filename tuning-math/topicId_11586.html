Here's a paper by a Slovenian electrical engineer, where he calculates
succesively higher values of |Z(t)|. He also notices that these come
near octave divisions, though without remarking that they are, in
fact, octave divisions. This is the first time I've noticed anyone
doing that.

http://lbk.fe.uni-lj.si/pdfs/mcom2004.pdf

You can extract the scale divisions out of the results he tabulates by
multiplying through by ln(2)/(2 pi). The 162 scale divisions he finds
range from 1 to 1094176093069, and required nine months of cranking on
his Pentium 4. The 1094176093069 division seems OK for the 99 limit,
which I am sure will make people ready to run out and put it to use.
