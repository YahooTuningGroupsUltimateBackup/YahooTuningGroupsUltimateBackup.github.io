A COB is an acronym for "combination product bag", where a bag, also
called a multiset, is a set with repetition. An example showing COBs
might be interesting is the product of any two members of the bag
<1,3,3,5> with itself. This gives the bag <3,3,5,9,15,15>, which
reduced to a set becomes {3,5,9,15}, which is an ASS. I'd call either
one the COB, unless it turns out to be important to iterate them.
