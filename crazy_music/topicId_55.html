Mary asked...

> What made you decide on the tuning you used for Midsummer Nights
> Dream. To me the tuning sounds very normal with just enough of the
> exotic to keep it interesting.

Exactly: slightly exotic. I didn't have enough leeway to use any tuning
VERY exotic, so I tried the early sketches in a couple different 1/4 comma
meantones, and 12tET, and my 12-of-17 setup. I liked the strident
"shimmering" 3rds and 5ths of 17tET for this. It sounded quite a bit
darker in meantone, with those stable 3rds and flat fifths.

Orphon Soul wrote...

> First it sounded like the music to a marital arts movie.
> Then it sounded a little like music to an old cowboy movie...

Heh heh... Yes. The play has sort of 3 kinds of people: fairies of
course, and some nobility, and some working class folks ("mechanicals").
This leads naturally to 3 big sections of the overture, and 3 themes for
different scenes of the play. The overture themes are ordered Fairies,
then Mechanicals, then the Court, leading into the first scene. I wanted a
sort of "squeegie" cow-poke quality to the Mechanicals music.

Rick
