Hi All,

A while back I stumbled on this and have found myself coming back to it again and again over the last few months.

I had been playing with the
2)5 1.3.17.19.29 Dekany (On 1.3)
and found it really lovely and useful.

One day
I felt as though I wanted
to make it easier to navigate
the sounds of the CPS
by choosing factors
which in some way
already incorporated
aspects of
each other

I took the orgiinal dekany (above)
and using those factors
I created this matrix:

--> 1
1 --> 3 (*)
3 --> 9 (3*3)
17--> 51 (17*3)
19--> 57 (19*3)
29--> 87 (29*3)

The left hand column
is the orginal factor
and the right hand column
is that factor
multiplied by the seed value
(marked here with an (*))

In this way then
I have created a new set of
factors that have something
of the [3] throughout them
and yet also carry another
distinct flavour in each.

The resulting
2)6 1.3.9.51.57.87 Pentadekany (On 1.3)
sounds great to my ears.

This process
can easily be applied to the formation
of other CPS.

Would be curious to hear what you think.
