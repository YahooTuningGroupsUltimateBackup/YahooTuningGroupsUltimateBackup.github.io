Just some speculations I had on chords I
was playing around with on my "rational guitar"
(and my Excel graphs):

14:17:21 [> 7:17:3*7] gives a very clear "minor" sound,
despite its very wide "minor 3rd" of 17:14 [= 336 c].
(Of course, 21:14 is the good old 3:2 "5th".)

Even 16:13 [= 359 c] sounds like a "minor 3rd".

21:26:32 [> 3*7:13:1] ~ "major chord"
26:21 "major 3rd" = 370 c
32:26 "minor 3rd" = 359 c
32:21 "5th" = 729 c

19:24:28 [> 19:3:7] ~ "major chord"
24:19 = 404 c
28:24 [= 7:6] = 267 c
28:19 = 671 c

13:10 [= 454 c] still sounds like a "major 3rd".

13:19 [= 657 c] can definitely function as a "5th"

13:16:19:24 [> 13:1:19:3] ~ "major 7th chord"

12:15:18:22 [> 3:15:9:11] ~ "major 7th chord"

24:13 [= 1061 c] is very close to 11:6 [= 1049 c]
