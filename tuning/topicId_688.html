The Southwest Chamber Music Society, an LA-area new music group, is putting
on a concert that includes the following:

Harry Partch Barstow (perhaps John Schneider's rendition?)
La Monte Young The Well-Tuned Piano (no idea how this will be realized)
Lou Harrison Suite for Violin and American Gamelan
Frederick Lesemann Nataraja for Prepared Piano (Rick's a good friend of
mine, and I am anxious to hear this piece, but he's never written any JI
music that I know of)

Plus traditional Javanese gamelan music (probably the group from CalArts).
they are performing Saturday the 20th at 5:30 (!) at the Zipper Concert
Hall near downtown LA and Tuesday the 23rd at 8:00 at the Armory Center in
Pasadena. Tickets are $20, $15 for seniors and $10 for students. I'll
probably try to make the Tuesday concert. Anyone else?

After receiving Dan Wolf's reply about a possible unauthorized performance
of The Well-Tuned Piano, I emailed someone I know in the Southwest Chamber
Music Ensemble to ask what the story is. Here is what she said:

> Actually, the LaMonte Young piece was scrapped from the program because
>he didn't respond to over 6 months of attempts at communication. Southwest
>also tried to substitute any other work of his choice on the program, but
>again got no response. So, we are instead doing an Ives group: several of
>the songs, plus the 4th violin sonata (no alternate tunings there; no
>lawyers...!)
> Hope you can attend some of these performances; the Lou Harrison
>gamelan/violin work should be stunning.

It's pretty disappointing, all the more so because they didn't replace it
with another JI piece. I'll still be there, though -- I wouldn't miss the
Harrison in any case.
