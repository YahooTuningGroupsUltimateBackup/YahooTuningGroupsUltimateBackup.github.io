Dear Jose Manuel Leon,

I have been trying to determine the underlying modal scales in Traditional
Turkish
Music for some time now. I realized in the beginning that the scales could
not be deterministic, that is, 'points' on the pitch axis. Instead, they
have to be non-deterministic scales in the form of 'distributions' along
the pitch axis. I have seen similar structures in Western Music. See the
work of Carl Seashore in 1930s at the University if Iowa alluding to
legendary virtuosos of non-fretted instruments, such as the violin,
deviating from the underlying scale by increments far beyond colorations
such as vibratos.

I am not sure what 'adaptive tuning' or 'tuning on the fly' means. It
'smells' like a non-deterministic tuning where the scale is kept 'natural'
in the sense of Pythagorean tuning at the 'local' level while using a
mathematically manageable scale, such as a well-tempered scale, at the
macro level.
