Toronto, Canada - The submission process is closing soon for bands
and artists who want to take part in the Molson Canadian Music Week
Festival and will run until the submission deadline of December 7th,
2000.

The festival, now entering it 11th year, spotlights new and
developing bands in more than 25 different venues in Toronto, with up
to five different bands each night in each venue from March 28 -
April 1, 2001.

Toronto's most popular clubs and music halls throw open their doors
to industry insiders and the general public to create an electrifying
musicfest of discovery. The talented unsigned bands are music to the
ears of A&R reps on the lookout for next year's stars. Whether it's
the next "big thing" or one of today's hottest concert hitmakers,
you'll find what your lookin' for at the Festival.

Long recognized as the most important event of its kind in Canada,
the Festival, a major part of Canadian Music Week International,
serves as a way for new artists to bring their music to the attention
of domestic and international industry professionals - including
agents, publishers, managers, record companies and talent bookers.
Past years' headliners and featured artists have included: Alanis
Morissette, Barenaked Ladies, Tal Bachman, 54-40, Sloan, Prozzak, The
Headstones, Jacksoul, Serial Joe, Soul Decision, The Rankin Family,
Colin James, Alannah Myles, Big Sugar, Great Big Sea, Natalie
MacMaster, Semisonic, The Moffats, Tom Cochrane, Blue Rodeo,Jewel, Kc
& JoJo, McMaster & James, Eagle Eye Cherry, and Shawn Mullins,
Matthew Good Band, Holly McNarland, Sky, and Choclair

The FUTURE STARS TALENT SEARCH is once again being sponsored by
FUTURE SHOP in conjunction with UNIVERSAL MUSIC CANADA. More than 250
artists will compete for the "Best of the Fest" Grand Prize of
recording time and national distribution with UNIVERSAL MUSIC CANADA.

Entry forms and more information can be obtained on-line:
http://www.cmw.net , by e-mail: festival@cmw.net or by contacting
Canadian Music Week directly at (905)858-4747.
