Harry Partch : A Biography by Bob Gilmore(1998) states
that Partch was gay, but kept it mostly to himself, except
occasionally to shock some acquaintance. Of course, it
would have been better to keep it quiet in those days
(McCarthy et al.). He was apparently quite shy among
men, and had some problems with his own sexuality
stemming from his mother's influences (had him
circumcised at age eight, without his father knowing,
and without Partch knowing what was happening --
a real castration anxiety), possible sterility. Sheds some
light on his "corporeality" vs. abstraction. (But the bio
doesn't go much beyond that, concentrates on the development
of his music). No indication that he had any sexual relationships
with women according to the bio (I'm up to his residence
in Petaluma, early 60s).
