In my enthusiasm to spread the word about a great musical event,
I was a little hasty and posted my review without waiting
to clarify a few facts.

A blank line in the review was supposed to give the name of
Ted Coons, President of the AFMM and Professor of Psychology
at NYU, who provided Johnny and the rest of us access to the
performance space.

Joji Yuasa is the correct name of the composer of the solo
clarinet piece: I had mistyped it in my review.

Comments from Johnny Reinhard:

> My criticism of your review... First, your readers didn't
> see the single page program I had to do the night before because
> of a computer problem. ...You needn't belabor obvious typos.
>
> Second, there is no experiment in using CDs to move between set
> pieces. In order to have an attention span 11 hours long, or
> even less, the music must appear still moving. Straight
> "non-live performer" music is like television to many. Silent
> moving of equipment would have had an adverse effect and people
> would have simply walked out of the microthon. You didn't
> mention the humidity of the day with the downpour.

The last sentence is a reference to the 'lack of oxygen' complaint.
Johnny is quite correct that I should have mentioned the terrible
weather we unfortunately had to contend with this year.

Correction from David Beardsley regarding his piece _Sonic Bloom_:

> Not a subharmonic tuning.

The scale is actually:
1/1 9/8 7/6 9/7 11/8 3/2 13/8 7/4

Dave's guitar was a Gibson L6S, used as a MIDI controller,
and the source of the sounds was a Korg 05R/W rack-mount synth.
