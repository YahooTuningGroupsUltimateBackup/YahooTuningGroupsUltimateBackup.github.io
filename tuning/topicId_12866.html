Hi Kyle. By '6L3s', Dan means that the periodic structure of the
scale can be ultimately reduced to a collection of 9 intervals
of two different step-sizes, 6 of them being the large (L) step,
and 3 of them being the small (s) step.

To help you understand, the ordinary plain-vanilla diatonic scale
with which we're all familiar is a '5L2s' system: 5 large steps
('whole-steps', tones) and 2 small steps ('half-steps', semitones).

Note that the periodic structure need not necessarily repeat at
the '8ve'. For an example of some that don't (with MIDI audio
files), see my webpage on 'Charles Ives's 1/8-tone scales':

http://www.ixpres.com/interval/monzo/ives/48tet.htm

My guess would be that Liszt used the '8ve' as his repeating basis,
but who knows? He was so experimental towards the end of his life
that maybe he too envisioned non-'8ve' scales.
