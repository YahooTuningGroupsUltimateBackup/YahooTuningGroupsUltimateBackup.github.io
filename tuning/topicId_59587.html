As an occasional reader of this group (and a long-time enthusiast of
alternative scales) I have read with interest the various posts that
reflect on the similarities between the sound spectrum and that of
vision / color / etc.

For those who also find this topic worth thinking about, I recommend
the latest issue of Scientific American magazine. SciAm is generally
a fairly hefty read, but they do try - on the whole - to make sure
that non-experts are able to walk away with some appreciation for the
subject matter of the article.

The article in question is entitled, "Is the Universe Out of Tune?"
The lede is, "Like the discord of key instruments in a skillful
orchestra quietly playing the wrong piece, mysterious discrepancies
have arisen between theory and observation of the 'music' of the
cosmic microwave background. Either the measurements are wrong or
the universe is stranger than we thought."

From there the authors go on to construct an elaborate, but
completely logical, analogy between the background of waves that are
the remnants of the Big Bang and are still echoing about the
universe, and a symphony orchestra. The link to the magazine is:

www.sciam.com

Scroll down to "Print Edition: August" and look for the headline
for "Cosmology: Is the Universe out of Tune."

I don't know how much longer the link will be there, so hurry if
you're interested.

Hope you enjoy.
