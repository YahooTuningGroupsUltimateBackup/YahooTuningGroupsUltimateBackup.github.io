Seventeen Tone Piano Project Phase Two
September 26, 8:00 PM
Hirsch Orchestra Rehearsal Hall
Shepherd School of Music, Rice University, Houston, Texas, USA

Program:

Of Silent Planet, Free Lancer  Mats Öljare
shine for thyself in thine own light  Erin Watson (*)
Ogives in a Winter Shelter Belt  John McLaird
Tres piano e forte  Yahya Abdal-Aziz
Waltz  Christopher Bailey
Etude  Jon Lyle Smith
Study  David Smith
Eighty-one ninth chords  Jacob Barton
Sub arbore  Margo Schulter ()
Sharks  Hans Straub
Cirrus and Pileus  Daniel Stearns (*)
past. in and. r. one. to  Taylan Susam
Three Lonely, Uninspired Ways to Play in 17tet  Thomas Edwin
Scheurich III

Jacob Barton, Dan Sedgwick, 17-tone pianos
(*) Ryan Stickney, voice
() Trio Life (Stephanie Nussbaum, Karen Raizen, David Gerstein)

This concert will be webcast live and afterwards. I will post URL to
webcast when I know it.

Jacob

Acoustic Instruments Make Microtonality Sound Correct.

Hey, Dan'l!

I just now listened to your cirrus & pileus performed LIVE! and it's
MAGNIFICENT!

It's works of this quality which will ensure that alternative
tunings find a place in the repertoire of professional musicians,
and in the hearts of listeners too.

Jacob put together an interesting and varied programme, of pieces
from guys who know what they're doing. I feel so honoured to be in
such company. But gee, as always, Margo's piece left me wanting
more ... guess I'll just have to put it on high rotation, eh?

Thank you to everyone who took part; it gives me great pleasure to
share your journeys along roads less travelled.

Best regards,
Yahya
