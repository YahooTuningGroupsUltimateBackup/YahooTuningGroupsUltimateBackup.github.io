>An extraordinary group of artists and dancers will gather at the
>Lorraine Hansberry Theater on May 31st and June 1st to celebrate and
>honor the work of one of the most important artists of our time.
>Pauline Oliveros. To celebrate her 70th birthday, some of the Bay Area's
>finest musicians and many internationally reknowned performers will perform
>a retrospective of her work from 1960 to the present in alarge orchestra and
>small group ensembles. On Friday, May 31st, at 8:00 p.m. the concert will
>include Four Meditations for Orchestra; Variations for Sextet, the Rova
>Saxophone Quartet performing Saxual Orientation, dancer Joanna Haigood and
>The Space Between, a trio with Oliveros on accordion, Philip Gelb on
>shakuhachi and Dana Reason, piano.
>
>On Saturday June 1st two performances will take place at the Lorraine
>Hansberry Theater, beginning at 1:00 p.m. and again at 8:00 p.m. During
>this five-set musical marathon, a four decade wealth of compositions
>will be performed includingTrio for Flute, Piano and Page Turner',
>'Double Basses at 20 Paces', excerpts from the opera Njinga the Queen
>King: Return of a Warrior, "The Well and the Gentle" and numerous other
>works. Musicians include pianist Sarah Cahill, violinist India Cooke,
>percussionist George Marsh, computer artist Chris Brown, the Cardew Choir,
>and the Deep Listening Band with Stuart Dempster, David Gamper and special
>guests..
>
>On June 2, the public is invited to share the Pauline Oliveros Birthday
>Potluck Picnic 1:00 p.m. in Dolores Park. Free participatory
>performance of the Grand Buddha Marching Band.
