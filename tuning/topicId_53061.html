The best of these scales seem clearly to be the second listed, which
has five each of subminor and supermajor triads, and four each of
chords of type 1-3/2-7/4 and 1-7/6-7/4. I called the best of the
64/63 and 729/686 blocks "aaron", so I think I'll call this "akj". It
has a ratio of largest to smallest scale step of 2.67, and a Lumma
stability of 0.223924. Once again, we have an improper scale but of
course epimorphic. As expected, the scale is more 3-oriented than
aaron.scl, with 3 exponents ranging from -3 to 4, and 7 exponents
only from -1 to 1, and with nine fifths this time. Aaron.scl seems to
be more what Aaron was looking for, but this also is an interesting
scale, and should be excellent for making Michael Harrison style
music.
