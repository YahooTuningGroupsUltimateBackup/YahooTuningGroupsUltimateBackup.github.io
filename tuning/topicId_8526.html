I am very interested in the whole Johnny Reinhard, Daniel Wolf
"controversy" concerning Andreas Werkmeister.

According to what I am understanding, Werkmeister was starting to think
about advocating equal temperament, but actually was using something else,
with the thirds closer to meantone, ie. a kind of "well temperament."

In this case, the "conventional knowledge" of the definitions in such
publications as the Harvard Dictionary of Music are not quite correct:

[Willi Apel, Harvard Dictionary on "temperament:"]

"Equal temperament is usually said to have been invented by Andreas
Werckmeister c. 1700..."

Nicht wahr??

Joseph Pehrson
