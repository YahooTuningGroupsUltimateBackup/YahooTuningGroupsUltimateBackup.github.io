G. Armellino, worte for master craftsman-tuners in his famous
standard textbook about practical temperament:
Kunst des Klavierstimmens (Art of piano tuning) Weimar 1881,p.36

"Der minder Geübte pflegt die Schwebung meistens zu übertreiben,
um sie deutlich zu erkennen, und bringt durch allzubedeutende
Schwächung der Quinten das entgegengesetzte Mißverhältniss von dem
hervor, was er ausgleichen soll."...
translation:
"The less trained layman overpronounces mostly the
beating (of the 5ths) to much broadly
in order to discern the beats (more) clearly,
(*for providing easier counting detection*)
and achieves by that an overprominent damping (flat) downwards
in the 5ths the reverse incongruent maladjustment
what he should counterbalance (inherently)."...
namely:
avoiding an final overwide wolf 5th residual at the end,
not to be confused with an wrong labeled
meantonic "diminished 6th wolf" closing rest.
