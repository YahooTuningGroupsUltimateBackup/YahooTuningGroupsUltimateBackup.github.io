hey guys,

you've both alluded to the question of a ji version of blackjack in the past.

after checking with kraig grady, i think perhaps the most solid answer is this:

/tuning/files/perlich/scales/blackjust4.gif

the consonances that are 'broken' in the ji tuning are shown in gray. they're
broken by the ratios indicated -- 224:225, 16875:16807, and 2400:2401, which
are only 7.7, 7.0, and 0.7 cents, respectively. if you can live with these
errors, this ji rendition gives you the full 7-limit resources of the tempered
blackjack (where the maximum error is 3 cents).

for convenience, here is 'blackjust4' in order (note that 1/1 is what we're
tuning to B[ (a quarter-tone below B) in the 'standard key') along with some
approximated ratios (differing from the main ratio by 7.7, 7.0, or 0.7 cents):
