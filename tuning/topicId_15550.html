Back in the Midwest, where I hails from, we like our overtone series
the "old fashioned way..." We like to hear them low integer
overtones of the series, none of this "high fallutin" NEW just
intonation practice with the 125th harmonic and a whole bunch of
difference tones. We call that "noise" in our parts...

Therebee, I would heartily recommend the "old style" just intonation
of a swift young lad called "Shri Swifty."

"Shri Swifty & the Mandali of Mantra" CD. Now HERE'S a harmonic
series drone I can recognize...

In fact, we have taken to listening to this soon after the rooster
crows in the barn, supplanting temporarily our usual diet of Perotin
or Renaissance newbies...

We didn't know Swifty had so many records out. Many are in "limited
release" which is a smart idea for "specialty items."

There's lots of stuff. Fortunately Swifty gave me a "sample CD" at
our recent square (believe it) dance and hoedown.

"Cherry Smash" records carries this old time stuff...

3322 Mentone Ave., Apt. 6
Los Angeles, CA 90034

Or just look online:

http://members.aol.com/cherrysmas/catalogue.html

(This catalogue is a little out of date... like two years... maybe
better to write...)

Our old style just intonation droners plan to purchase several of
these CDs, except for "Fluffy Kitty... Give Us Kisses..."

That one can wait... at least for the time being...
