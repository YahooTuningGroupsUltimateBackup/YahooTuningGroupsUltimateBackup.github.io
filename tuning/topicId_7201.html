Joe Pehrson wrote,

>Looking forward to some further explanation of pivot "homophones" in 12-tET
>as contrasted/compared with the "homophones" of JI...

As usually considered, JI doesn't have any homophones. Unison vectors (very
small JI intervals) lead to exceptions, but then you're really using JI as a
temperament, and once you've crossed that line, distributing the unison
vectors out by tempering the consonances by fractions of the unison vectors
generally seems desirable.

>Can there really be traditional "pivot modulation" or functional harmony in
>"adaptive" Just Intonation?

Absolutely. Strict JI poses greater problems here.
