What I did (in LA) was to just go to all the best luthier/repair people I could find, and ask them if if they would do it. The guy who did mine (John Carruthers) had a CNC fret-slotting saw for cranking out bolt-on necks. He also had a 'copying lathe' and all kinds of different neck templates, so he gave me a new neck in the shape I liked best with the tuning I specified for the same price as a good re-fret--no partial or bent frets, though. So, that's for a Strat or Tele style...What about custom ordering just a neck from Music Man?

Of the luthiers I spoke to, only a rabid experimentalist like Rick Turner expressed any interest (to me), and he asked me to bring him a guitar without a fingerboard, which he said he would be happy to provide in whatever tuning I desired. (But I promised not to ask for partial frets there too, I think!) I was going to order a new guitar from Taylor, and specify 'w/o fingerboard', but it fell through (the gig with the the endorsement deal, that is).

Rod Poole's experience would be good to find out, too, as would John Schneider's...

Cheers!
