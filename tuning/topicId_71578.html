I'm curious if anyone has made (mp3/ogg/etc.) files of Gesualdo's
vocal music - scores here

http://www.icking-music-archive.org/ByComposer/Gesualdo.php

The most 'extreme' example of chromaticism is usually held to be 'Moro
lasso' from the Sixth Book. In general I don't think Gesualdo goes
beyond the Gb-B# range of a 19-note keyboard or uses enharmonic steps.
I would be interested to hear how it sounds in 1/4 comma or 31-equal,
which are both good easy approximations to whatever sort of JI one
might hold to be ideal for the music.

As usual with Renaissance vocal music, harmonic-rich and vibratoless
timbres are highly preferred. For example the reed organ known as
'regal' - essentially an early harmonium.
