> http://bach.tuning.googlepages.com/literature

If you like that one, don't miss his *first* (and most original and enlightening) piece along that line, 6/24/2004, "The Keyboard Temperament of J. S. Bach":
http://www.eunomios.org/contrib_index.html

That's the one that derives a 1/3 syntonic comma system esoterically from mordents in a C-major piece. Interestingly, not included in his own "literature" page at his later googlepages!

The editorial policy of that hosting depository,
http://www.eunomios.org/
says: "EUNOMIOS hosts user-contributed papers on topics concerning theory, analysis, and semiotics of music. Submission is completely free, and publication on the site will occur immediately, without any editing or peer review." There's also a forum there (really more like a guestbook) for discussing the submissions.

The 4/18/2006 one deposited by him is also illuminating, with its 60-item list of "references" almost all by Kellner.
