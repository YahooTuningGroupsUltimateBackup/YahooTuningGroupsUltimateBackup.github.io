Here is the news release as posted on "rec.music.classical.contemporary".
There was quite a bit of discussion of Xenakis on Usenet the past few weeks;
most of it was saying that he had Alzheimer's and had stopped composing a
few years ago. My own memories of his music are rather hazy. He was
certainly attuned to the Tetrachord and to Byzantine/Eastern Orthodox
ecclesiastical scales. There is a fairly large section in "Formalized Music"
on these subjects, and at one time he was probably the foremost authority on
them. I first discovered Xenakis about the same time I discovered Hiller,
Partch and Nikolais -- when I was an undergraduate (1959 - 1962). From the
dates given in the news release, he may have actually pre-dated Hiller in
algorithmic composition. In some sense, I think pure stochastic music is a
dead end, and it took someone of his stature to give it any viability.
Still, he was a genuine pioneer -- arrows in the back and all -- and he will
be remembered fondly by all who are interested in "new music".
