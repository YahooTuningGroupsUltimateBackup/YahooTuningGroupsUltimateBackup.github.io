As a coincidence this came on in my car on my way home from
a friend's tonight. I thought it made a very interesting
example of functional harmony. And because it's not available
on your precious internet, I've stayed up late ripping it
for you

http://lumma.org/stuff/Bohm-GelobetSeistDuJesuChrist.mp3

It's a theme and variations on a Lutheran chorale by one
Georg Bohm, sometime teacher of JS Bach. The genre is known
as Stylus Phantasticus, which is Latin for rock and roll.

So yeah, I'd call it functional, but it still has hints of
noodling around. I'm going to say it sounds a bit "Petr
functional" to me. Petr's chord progressions (and Herman's)
are the most natural I've heard in alternative regular
temperaments, and it seems like this is something Petr might
play for his meantone example were meantone new and strange.

Penny for your thoughts,

-Carl
