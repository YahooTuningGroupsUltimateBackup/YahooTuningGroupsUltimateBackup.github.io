Over on tuning-math we found a "spooky" periodicity in the graph
of "good" ETs for the 7-limit. The graph seemed to cycle from high to
low to high again every 1664 ETs.

Mystified, we looked at 5-limit, and found the same pattern every 612
ETs.

Familiar with 612-tET as the good old "scale of schismas", it hit be
that there must be an important 7-limit tiny interval that is 1/1664
octave.

Indeed there is: 2401:2400, with which Graham Breed created the now
famous "Blackjack pump" chord progression some time ago . . .

What stopped us is that 1664-tET does not itself look like an
especially good 7-limit ET, nor does anything in its vicinity . . .

. . . more useless theory news over at tuning-
math@yahoogroups.com . . .

More useless theorizing...

Here's the prime-factorization of the 2401:2400 ratio:

2^-5 * 3^-1 * 5^-2 * 7^4

So in "8ve"-invariant terms (i.e., ignoring prime-factor 2),
it's the difference between 7^4 (= 2401:2048 = ~275.3036259 cents)
and 3^1 * 5^2 (= 75:64 = ~274.5824286 cents), which amounts to
only ~0.721197281 (just a bit more than 5/7) cent.
