Hey, has anyone ever noticied this before? This is fun!

http://www.math.niu.edu/~rusin/uses-math/music/41tone.com

It's a little program that turns your computer keyboard into
a 41-EDO keyboard instrument. The mapping is cleverly
arranged so that a row of keys emulates the 12-tone scale
and the rows under it raise the pitches by one 41-EDO degree:

40 0 3 7 10 14 17 21 24 27 31 34 38 41
1 4 8 11 15 18 22 25 28 32 35 39
2 5 9 12 16 19 23 26 29 33 36 [40]
x 6 x 13 x 20 x x 30 [x] 37

where "40" is mapped to the "~" key, "0" to the "1" key,
"1" to the "Q" key, etc. "x" means that that key doesn't
do anything, and can be used to simply turn off the sound.
SHIFT makes the note an "8ve" higher. Hitting SPACE twice
exits the program.

The Pascal source code is here:
http://www.math.niu.edu/~rusin/uses-math/music/41tone.pas

and also explains how it looks if you have a Dvorak keyboard.

With a little tweaking, this could easily be transformed
into the 41-tone MIRACLE tuning. (How come we never found
a card game to name that one after?)

Enjoy!
