I was lucky enough to pick up an 12 chord Autoharp Zither for $42 (including shipping) from Ebay (new these are > $200).

Picture, example song, and 12 edo tuning information (jpegs)are here:

http://notonlymusic.com/board/viewtopic.php?f=11&t=803&start=0

Now, of course I'd like to microtune this baby. However, this poses an interesting challenge because of how it works. One is suppose to / can strum the entire width of the autoharp and chords are formed by pressing felt dampers onto the strings to silence the unwanted notes. So... the A notes get used in A7, Dm, Am, D7 and need a working relationship with the other members of those chords.

So, while I'm used to the idea of taking a tuning and finding a set of chords from it I wonder if anyone here has done the reverse - that is taken a set of desires chords and worked out a non-12 edo tuning that makes them all usable.

The 12 chords of my autoharp zither are:

Gm, Bb, A7, C7, Dm, F, E7, G7, Am, C, D7, G
