Congratulations Joseph! Frank Oteri did a little
feature on me too, last year, when my music drama From
The realm Of The Shadow came out (Naxos 8.559089-90, 2
CDs, see www.fromtherealmoftheshadow.com). Frank Oteri
is deeply interested in alternative tunings and
actually composed a 31 note to the octave opera. My
piece sounds really traditional most of the way, but
has a 19 note to the optave section near the end as
well as a 34-note to the octave oud playing a
middle-eastern inspired piece in 21/8 time (that's the
part he excerpted). I guess as a composer I went
straight from the 18th-19th century styles to the 21st
century, and skipped the 20th almost entirely. Weird.
I'm glad Sibelius works for you; I guess I should make
the investment in it myself for my upcoming 53 note to
the octave oratorio. By the way, the sounds I plan to
use are with Gigastudio, which allows microtonal
tunings but only very grudglingly. I'll have to put in
300,000 manual commands just to get Garritan Strings
into the system in 53-equal! Arrgh. BUT, version 3.0
is coming out, and they're considering creating the
ability to generate custom tuning macros somehow.
A favor I'm asking anyone and everyone. Even if you're
not into Gigastudio, could you ask if they are
considering a microtonal music feature for their
digital sampling software?
Thanks, and kudos again!
Chris Mohr
