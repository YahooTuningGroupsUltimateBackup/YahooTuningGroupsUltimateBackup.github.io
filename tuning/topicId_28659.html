There are actually two common meanings of the word freeware.
Scala is freeware in the sense that it costs nothing and I've been
using the word in that sense myself. But in case of GNU and other
software it has an entirely different meaning, namely that you are
free do to almost anything with the software code, including changing
it or ask money for it. With Scala it's certainly not allowed to
ask money for it for one thing.

See http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/words-to-avoid.html for more
explanation.
