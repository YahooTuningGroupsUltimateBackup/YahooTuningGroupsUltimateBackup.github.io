Hi Oz;

Let's not have a flame war about this.

My comments on the list re. religion were in response to someone who
had posted here suggesting that my interest in musical tuning was
influenced by my religious point of view.

This reminded me of an interview that I had done on BBC Radio 4 a
couple of years ago.

The interview is linked here:

http://www.lucytune.com/mp3/OutOfTune2.mp3

This interview and the musical examples were recorded in our flat in
London, and whilst the presenters were here, my wife (who is a
Catholic) commented that John Harrison was devoutly religious.

(BTW Harrison also wrote very disparagingly about clerics in a couple
of his books and manuscripts - see the transcriptions).

That Harrison was very religious was announced by the presenter which
made it sound as though my interest in Harrison was also in some way
religious.

This was/is/prolly never will be the case; my interest was/is only
musical and scientific, yet the BBC audience could have been misled
due the presenter regurgitating my wife's comments at the time.

Having had this unfortunate experience with people suggesting that I
had a religious motive, when I saw the comment on the list today I
responded to clarify that I have no religious affiliation,

and to state my own perspective and prejudices about religion.

Although I fail to believe in any particular religion, nevertheless I
appreciate the cultural, ethical and moral influences which religious
organisations and beliefs have had and continue to have on

human societies and consciousness.

So let's not argue about religion - frankly I find the whole subject
of religion boring.

Vive peace and harmony ;-)

Charles Lucy lucy@lucytune.com
