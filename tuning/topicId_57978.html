The Just Intonation Network Presents Terry Riley--Recent Solo Keyboard and Vocal Works

Location: Saint Gregory of Nyssa Episcopal Church, 500 De Haro Street, San Francisco

Date/Time: Saturday April 23, 8PM

Tickets: $20 General Admission, $18 Students, Seniors and Just Intonation Network Members: via the web at www.justintonation.net/concerts.html, at the door, or by mail from 535 Stevenson Street, San Francisco CA 94103 (make checks payable to the Just Intonation Network)

Contact: Carola Anderson 415-864-0411 / carola@justintonation.net

For the second program in our series, minimalist, world music, and Just Intonation pioneer Terry Riley, who celebrates his seventieth birthday in 2005, performs recent works for solo electronic keyboard and voice, including *The Dream* and *Beat Sutras*.

*The Dream*, the third in a series of pieces for solo keyboard using Just Intonation, was created in October and November 1999 for Riley's last tour of the millennium. The Dream follows The Harp of New Albion and is the composer's most significant work for keyboard in Just Intonation to date.

The texts for the *Beat Sutras*, sung in a style derived from North Indian classical music, draw on the beat poetry of Jack Kerouac and Michael McClure, who were two of the original beat poets from that 1950s movement. They, along with Gary Snyder, were among the first American poets to introduce Buddhist teachings in their writings and poetry.
