Speaking of 'old wine in new bottles'...

I've just finished re-tuning what I've long thought is
my best piece, a 'smooth-jazz'-type song called _3 Plus 4_.

I wrote it in 12-EDO/12-tET back in January of 1992, and
have just redone it in 13-limit JI. The basic set of pitches
is 5-limit, with a few higher-prime ratios here and there.

I'll make an analytical webpage of it within the near future.
Here's the MIDI-file:

http://www.ixpres.com/interval/monzo/3plus4/3plus4ji.mid

The two solos were played on the original MIDI sequence
by my friend Marc Johnson (no, not the famous jazz bassist),
and were retuned by me yesterday.

The original 12-EDO version is available here:

http://www.ixpres.com/interval/monzo/3plus4/3plus4.mid

but be forewarned: the tempo and mix on this version
are not quite right.

In any case, I think that in every way the new JI version
is far superior to the old one, and is without any doubt
the best thing I've written so far.

Enjoy!
