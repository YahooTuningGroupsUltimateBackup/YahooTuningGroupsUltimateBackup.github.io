In arithematical series with a constant denominatore ( otonal systems )
which I call them "" increasing/constant rational system "" , the
denominator is like the cardinality of EDO system , showing the number
of degrees in system( denominator+1). so the chords for example 4:5:6
are related to a i/c rational or otonal system of:

4/4 5/4 6/4 7/4 8/4 . if we accept octave equivalency , it will be
simplified as : 1/1 5/4 3/2 7/4 2/1 so the real numerary nexus ,
according to my view , is 4 .

In the utonal system , ''''constant/decreasing rational system "" , with
constant nominator = 8 we have so : 8/8 8/7 8/6 8/5 8/4 and after
o.e we have : 1/1 8/7 4/3 8/5 2/1 so the real n.n is 8 and 8=2*4

Yes , good idea for homogenizing, but this system is a way of for
example equally-dividing string and nominator is the number of
divisions. in this 8-rdo(c/d) or utonal system you divide string into 8
equal segments and 8/7 means that 7 segments of string is vibrating.

We can costruct these two systems also with superparticular ratio:

1- y/b ------ a/x (y/b < a/x , y=2x) , now costruct system from 1/1 with
numerary nexus = y , you will have utonal system

2- a/x ------ y/b (y/b < a/x , y=2x) , now costruct system from 1/1 with
numerary nexus = x , you will have otonal system
