Herman Miller has recorded a number of sequences, both of his own music
and others', which I am long, LONG overdue in getting up on my web site.
I have gone back and collected up everything he's sent me (Herman, tell
me if I've missed any!), and run them all through the latest version of
the program. The file is hmiller.zip, on my web site:

http://www.idcomm.com/personal/jadl/

Each piece is presented in four tunings:

xxx.mid: original 12-tET sequence
xxxc5.mid: 5-limit Calculated Optimum Fixed Tuning (COFT)
xxxcs5.mid: 5-limit adaptive tuning, grounded to COFT.
xxxcs7.mid: 7-limit adaptive tuning, grounded to COFT.

The sequences are as follows:

dragons: The Dragons of Torennilan, Copyright 1984 H.Miller.

galticeran: The Black Caves, Copyright 1986 H.Miller.

rriladeni-harp: Copyright 1987 H.Miller.

Er549: a Scarlatti sonata.

Praelud7:
Fugue7: Bach Prelude & Fugue no. 7 (WTK, book I).

chopin-waltz-c-sharp-minor: Chopin Waltz in C# minor.

moonlight-1:
moonlight-2:
moonlight-3: Beethoven's Moonlight sonata.

maple-leaf-rag: Scott Joplin's Maple Leaf Rag.

For more pieces by Herman Miller, see his web page:

http://www.io.com/~hmiller/music/music.html
