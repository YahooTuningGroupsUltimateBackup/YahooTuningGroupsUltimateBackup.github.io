I Gede Sanat Kumara wrote:
> Anybody interested in Gamelan ? Yes, very much so. I became interested back in the late 1980's when I heard some Balinese gamelan music from an old Nonesuch recording in the music library at Michigan State University. (Also around that time, a year or so later, Wendy Carlos' album _Beauty in the Beast_ came out, with one track that featured electronic simulations of gamelan instruments playing along with instruments of the Western symphony orchestra.)

I've heard enough recordings to have a good general idea of the pelog and slendro tunings, but each gamelan has its own unique tuning. It's a very interesting tradition.

> Anybody interested in Gamelan ?

Yes - Gamelan is a wonderful tradition. I performed in a gamelan
ensemble in Pittsburgh for several years, performing mostly Sundanese
and some Javanese as well under ethnomusicologist Andrew Weintraub. I
also had the lovely opportunity to perform with Ismet Ruchimat, Ati
Sumiati, Undang Sumarna, Burhan Sukarma, Gangan Garmana, Euis
Komariah, and dancer Ben Arcangel.
Gamelan is one of the most beautiful traditions of music in the world,
in my opinion. A very unique and noble music. Wayang golek is
incredible as well. There are even a few pieces of wayank golek
performance on youtube.com.
