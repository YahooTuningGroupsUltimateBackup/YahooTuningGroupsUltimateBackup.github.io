Hi all,

I've been playing around in Graham's temperament finder. Searching the
2.3.7.13/5 (actually 2.3.7.78/5*) subgroup for rank 3 temperaments
yields the following two at the top of the badness list:

http://x31eq.com/cgi-bin/rt.cgi?ets=31_5_19&limit=2_3_7_78%2F5
http://x31eq.com/cgi-bin/rt.cgi?ets=22_5_19&limit=2_3_7_78%2F5

The second is biome, which equates 9/7 with 13/10. The first is an
unnamed temperament that instead goes the other direction and equates
13/10 with 21/16 by eliminating 105/104 - certainly a very interesting
approach! I'm not sure exactly how it might be used, although it makes
possible chords like 7/4-8/7-13/10-7/6 that span a 3/1 on the outside.
You can play around with subminor 11 chords and then "resolve" them
into 10:12:13:15:18 chords. Not as conceptually simple as 1/(6:7:9) ->
10:13:15, but interesting nonetheless.

Does anyone know of any good linear temperaments that eliminate
105/104? Mixing this with 91/90 gives you a negri-ish temperament,
which isn't as accurate as either since the two commas drag the size
of the 7 in opposite directions. I guess 36-equal and 60-equal are
about optimal for this?

-Mike

*This subgroup is equivalent to 2.3.7.13/5, but I'm using it to weight
Graham's temperament finder properly.
