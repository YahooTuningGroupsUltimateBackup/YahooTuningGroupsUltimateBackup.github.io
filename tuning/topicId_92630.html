>> practitioners, namely, Ishaq al-Mawsili (d. 850) and his followers.

>> in four treatises of al-Kindi (d. after 870) and four treatises of
>> al-Farabi (d. 950). The latter created a precise notation system,
>> and constructed an unparalleled theory of Middle Eastern rhythms by
>> relying on his own experience as a performer and by using models
>> from the works of Euclid, Aristotle, Aristoxenus, and Arab scholars
