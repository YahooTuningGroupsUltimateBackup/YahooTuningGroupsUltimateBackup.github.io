Over Thanksgiving holiday, I worked on enhancing my adaptive retuning
program so as to be able to handle sequences with multiple voices. The
preliminary results I'm hearing are quite exciting!

The bad news: I'm still using pitch bending, to tap into the General
MIDI standard as before, which has no provision for tuning tables. This
does put channels at a premium, and means that very dense sequences
can't be accommodated. BUT, by dynamically reassigning channels to the
voices currently in use, I've been pleasantly surprised to find that
some fairly dense tunes DO fit! I'll post some examples soon.

An interesting observation so far: I'm going to tighter vertical
springs than I usually use for piano voice alone, and finding, to my
ear at least, the resulting horizontal motions not to be troubling. I
don't know why this is, and don't know if others will share my
observations.

Monz, I've got the Schoenberg retuned to 7 and to 13; shall I send these
to you? I think both sound nice!

I invite anyone with multiple-voice GM midi sequences to forward them to
me, to see how well the program does.
