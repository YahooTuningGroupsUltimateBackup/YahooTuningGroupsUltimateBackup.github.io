> Rolands tend to be among the least flexible, tuning-wise.

I don't think that's true. Hardware synths are all about
the same these days at the manufacturer level, with the
possible exception of Kurzweil (though they're sort of just
coming back from the dead at the moment). No synth offers
what I'd call flexible tuning control. Virtually all Roland
instruments support arbitrary 12-tone tunings.

Microtonal-synthesis.com is a great (and the only) resource
for this stuff, but its grid doesn't capture the important
nuances of tuning capabilities/limitations, and sometimes the
information there is just wrong (obtaining it from
manufacturers is nearly impossible; manuals often don't
mention it; often the only way to find out is to test the
instrument in person, which, with the demise of showrooms,
is nearly impossible). And it only lists a minority of
available instruments.

-Carl
