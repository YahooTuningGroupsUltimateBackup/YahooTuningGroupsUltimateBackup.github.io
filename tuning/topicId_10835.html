For the moment, I would like to dissipate only a misunderstanding. When I
use the term "gamme" in french (that I translate by "scale") I don't refer
to the standard meaning which is much concerned with fixed notes, tuning,
acoustic, transposition, interfaces, etc.

As music is a human communication (and expression) I want to describe the
implicit conditions of intelligibity. The tonal segment of this
intelligibility depend mostly, in my opinion, of the only elementary
invariance of time & frequency thru acoustical channel which permit
algebrical structures (concerning time & frequency) to serve as paradigm in
(schematic) form recognition. If you name "ideal" the conception of
Zarlino, you have to go up for qualify mine's.

You presume, because I was speaking only of JI structure, that I reject
using of temperament. It will be odd after I had severely criticized, in
"Fétichisme du numérique et rationalité musicale" the use of obsessive
precision. You will never find number like 234.567891234567 in my texts.
It's absolutely ridiculous. There is no irrationality in perception. There
are two rationnal dimensions : rationality of widths (0 1 2 3 4 ..) and
rationality of sonances (2/1 3/2 4/3 ..). Actual mathematics join in real
field the two perceptive dimensions, inserting irrationality. It's good for
acoustical description of sonic matter. True tonal matter need a more
sophisticated mathematical approach respecting the concern of musical
apprehension. At end, If you think I reject temperament look at

http://www.aei.ca/~plamothe/jchal/jchalmers.htm

I stop here. I could write ten pages with fine shades in french in the time
I can write this approximation of my thoughts.
