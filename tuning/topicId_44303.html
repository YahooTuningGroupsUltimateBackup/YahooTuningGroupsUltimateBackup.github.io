Of particular note, at least to me, are the "hidden crosses" of
the "otonal augmagic" and "utonal augmagic" chords in Blackjack.

If both otonal and utonal elements are added, it's a "cross like"
structure.

It sounds great, but one has to be careful in the voicing.

For example: E>:Ab^:C:D:D< contains *both* the otonal D and the
utonal D<.

Since they are quite close together the create quite a dissonance,
but if voiced in different octaves the effect is (subjectively) nicer
or at least doesn't seem to have an "out of place" dissonance...
