we've all heard of the 31-tone keyboards of vicentino, gonzaga,
stella, and colonna.

the other day i opened up the _companion to medieval and renaissance
music_ and saw mention of vito trasuntino's 1606 "clavemusicum
omnitonum" also with 31 tones. supposedly it was tuned by means of
a "tetrachord", like a monochord but with four strings.

according to this webpage:
http://music.cwru.edu/duffin/Vallotti/T1/55division.html
this is another example of 1/4-comma meantone extended to create a
nearly closed system.

anyone have any information on the "tetrachord"?
