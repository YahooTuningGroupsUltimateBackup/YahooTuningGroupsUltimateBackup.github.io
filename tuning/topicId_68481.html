My apologies if my recent postings have struck
a majority of list members as being "out of line".

I'm 58 years old, homeless, unemployed, and living
on the street, and don't even have a set of headphones,
let alone an AM/FM radio or anything else musical other
than an occasional trip to the synthesizer section of
Best Buy or guitar center to play their synths.

That's the primary reason I'm "stuck" in
meta-theoretical/analytical pre-compositional
music theory space. :)

But it's also no surprise that pre-compositional
music theory is not accorded the level of respect
that it deserves in the musical "culture" (and I use
that word extremely loosely! :) ).

As if a bunch of MIDI files of reference melodies
are not as important as another silly-assed generic
dumb-flock "subjective" composition.

Sorry folks, I regard both 20th century modernist
and postmodernist composers to have utterly failed
in their responsibility to the evolution of mainstream
music theory and education.
