Here are the scales of five through nine tones that result from a
Phi-weighted MOS generator where L/s always equals phi... these scales
could be seen as those that occupy the business district of what I
would call -- after Thorvald Kornerup's "Golden Meantone" --
generalized golden meantones; though it has been pointed out by Paul
Erlich and Kraig Grady (via private email), that this idea is
essentially synonymous with Erv Wilson's "Golden Horagrams of the
Scale Tree", see:

<http://www.anaphoria.com/hrgm01.html>

The scale examples below are given in an Ls form that has the
generator rotated back to the tonic position -- this would be the
middle row of each example; the top row is the scales step-structure
"key", and the last row is the scales interval matrix separated by
scale class.

The algorithm I used is:

X = P/(N*D)

"P" = periodicity, "N" = (b+phi*d) and "D" = (a+phi*c) where a/b, c/d
are the adjacent fractions, and "X" = the corresponding phi-weighted
MOS generator.
