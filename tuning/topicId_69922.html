The flat octaves are Gene's idea, really. He just convinced
me to try them out. And they work well. So far I've only
given them a fair shake on synths -- to put them on a piano,
I'm thinking beat rates must be used (I'm currently working
on a bearing plan).

The trines with brats of +2 are my doing, though it was
Gene who pointed out that because of the flat octaves making
good triadic brats in one octave won't transfer to other
octaves, and therefore that trinic brats were a good idea.

By the way, I don't know if I ever published Alaska7. It's
definitely my best circulating flat-octaves tuning.
