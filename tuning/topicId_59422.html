One measure I would propose of how 'easy' or 'efficient' a comma can be
produced, 'commatic efficiency':

In the 7-limit, we take a 'decrease' in the size of the minimum 'comma'
available for a 'taxicab distance' of 1 (8/7 in the 7-limit) minus our target
comma, and divide that 'gain' by the 'pain' of getting there (the number of
'taxicab steps' to produce it).

I hope that wasn't too confusing....

formula:

commatic_efficiency"=(smallest_available_interval_in_given_limit_by_taxicab_distance_of_1-target_interval)/target_interval_taxicab_distance

so for the 7-limit, if we sort by decreasing commatic efficiency, we see that
64/63 is the most efficient:
