Just Intonation Network 20th Anniversary Series Concert Three: "Made in California"--John Schneider plays works in Just Intonation for solo guitar, voice, and adapted viola by California composers David Doty, Lou Harrison, Harry Partch, Terry Riley, and John Schneider

Location: Noe Valley Ministry, 1021 Sanchez Street, San Francisco

Date/Time: Saturday April 30, 2005, 8PM

Tickets: $20 General Admission, $18 Students, Seniors and Just Intonation Network Members: via the web at www.justintonation.net/concerts.html, at the door or by mail from 535 Stevenson Street, San Francisco CA 94103 (make checks payable to the Just Intonation Network)

Contact: Carola Anderson: 415-864-0411 / carola@justintonation.net

Internationally known guitarist and Just Intonation specialist John Schneider performs a program of twentieth and twenty-first century music in Just Intonation for a variety of uniquely fretted guitars, guitar and voice, and adapted viola and voice. The program includes works by Just Intonation pioneer Harry Partch (1901-1974), including Barstow (1941) and seven of his Li Po Settings (1933), which feature a replica of Partch's "Adapted Viola" (a viola with a lengthened neck, played cello fashion) and intoning voice. Also featured are several works by Lou Harrison (1917-2003), including Scenes from Nek Chand (2002) for Just National Steel Guitar, one of Harrison's last completed works; Quando Cosas Malas caen del Cielo (2002) by minimalist and world music pioneer Terry Riley (b. 1935); the premier of a new work by Just Intonation Network cofounder David Doty (b. 1950), and Schneider's own Lament (1981) for Pythagorean classical guitar.
