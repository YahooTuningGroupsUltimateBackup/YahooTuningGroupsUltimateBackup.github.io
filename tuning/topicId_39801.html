Phillips All-key Harmonic-Partial Septimal 19-Limit (C) Just-Intonation

Copyright © by Pauline W. Phillips

22 notes to the octave, less Gb (in C) and the octave.

The key-signature root cents show why the scales need to have the octave
divided into 12 equal parts in the ET manner, so Db is 100 cents, D 200, etc.
Roots C, F, G and Bb indicate this tempering is the right procedure. As the
exact cents a key signature root begins on +/- a few cents in relation to an
adjacent key-signature root is musically immaterial. This greatly reduces the
number of notes needed!

It also enables one to use frequency-divider tone generators for both
key-signature roots and their slightly tempered 3rds, 5ths and harmonic minor
7ths, when the notes are created electronically.

*Gb is the 13th harmonic's harmonic minor 7th. Thus the 13th harmonic is used
indirectly. The 11th harmonic isn't needed. The missing A 886 cents
382725/262144 belongs to the F scale.

The septimal c minor scale (7c) uses underlined 7Eb and 7Ab. The tertian e
minor scale (e) uses underlined A#. The above tuning provides for all three
scales--C, 7c and e. It this were not done, many additional scales would have
to be provided! My just-temperament version merges these notes with their #
or b counterparts.
