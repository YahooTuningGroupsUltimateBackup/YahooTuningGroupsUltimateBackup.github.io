Hi Kraig,
I've tried to look at the major scale in this way some time ago, but had
serious trouble accepting 15 as logical.
before 15 you get 7 11 13 etc.
15 to me looks logical only if you see it as a 5 on 3, or as a 3 on 5.

One could see the major scale as 3 major triads.
1/1 5/4 3/2, 4/3 5/3 2/1, 3/2 15/8 9/4 giving 1/1 9/8 5/4 4/3 3/2 5/3 15/8
2/1

And if you make the major triads into minor triads:
1/1 6/5 3/2, 4/3 8/5 2/1, 3/2 9/5 9/4 giving 1/1 9/8 6/5 4/3 3/2 8/5 9/5 2/1

But I feel this way of looking at major and minor does not do music justice.
I now see the major and minor scale as the result of many different
underlying structures.

The most simple structures I'll past below:
