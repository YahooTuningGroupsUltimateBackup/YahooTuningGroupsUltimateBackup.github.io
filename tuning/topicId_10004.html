Starting from a position of looking for "nice chords" in 13 equal is
probably going to lead to frustration for most anyone... I generally
use these scales from a melodic standpoint first, and let the
'harmonies' take care of themselves; see "With Eyes So Blue And
Dreaming" for an example of this:

<http://stations.mp3s.com/stations/55/117_west_great_western.html>

Though others may not, I guess I personally have a very high tolerance
for the resultant 'incidental harmonies' that arise from going about
things in this way, and in fact, one of my very favorite things about
using equal tunings *is* the various distortions of consonances that
can arise, as they can also give the familiar new and striking
emotional nuances, and thereby (to my mind anyway) new compositional
potentials.

> Does anyone have some suggestion on what tones go together with 13 tet?
Alita Morrison

I like to think of it as every other note of 26-tET, which is consistent
though the 13-limit and also a meantone (4/9-comma). In 13-tET, you can't
get 26-tET's approximations of the 3rd harmonic or the 7th harmonic, but you
can get a full 4:5:9:11:13 chord, as well as (obviously) subsets and
subharmonic versions. The intervals in this chord are approximated about as
well as the intervals in 4:5:6 are approximated by 12-tET.

The 4:5:9:11:13 chord is 0 4 15 19 22 in 13-tET. Try it!
