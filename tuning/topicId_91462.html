37/26 is conflated with 3/2? Not possible; that's either a typo or simply the wrong ratio. 37/26 would be G!!/ or F/||\, which would be conflated with 729/512 (difference of -0.914 cents). To distinguish intervals this close, you need extreme (olympian-level) precision:

37/26: F/||\.. G!!/''

You'll have to decide if you really need to make a distinction of less than half of a schisma when you'd be hard pressed to play that accurately on a flexible-pitch acoustic instrument.

Anyway, here are the odd harmonics 25 thru 47 of G in extreme-precision sagittal:

25: D)||( E'\!!!
27: E
29: F(|' G)\!!!/
31: F~|||). G(\!.
33: G/|\ A'(!!!(
35: G.//|||' A\!)
37: A)//|'' B\\!!!
39: A.|). B(!/.
41: B/| C')!!(
43: C~|(..
45: C||\ D'\!!/
47: C/|||\' D~!)..

If you don't like all those tiny accent marks "adorning" your manuscript, you can try "set notation saji2" in Scala, which in most cases will give you the same notation as above, with the accent marks omitted.

I hope this helps.
