CHARLESTON, Illinois, USA (February 1, 2007)

H-Pi Instruments microtonal products to be demonstrated at UK Microfest
2 and Londonmet.

Aaron Andrew Hunt, director of H-Pi Instruments, will demonstrate the
Tuning Box microtonal MIDI keyboard converter, the Tonal Plexus
microtonal keyboard synthesizer, and MegaScore microtonal notation and
sequencing software, as part of a multimedia presentation "For the
Future of Music" at the three-day UK Microfest 2, March 4, and at the
one-day Londonmet experimental musical instruments festival, March 5,
2007. The Tuning Box TBX1 is scheduled to begin shipping two weeks
following these events.
1 UK MICROFEST 2

WHERE: riverhouse (half an hour from London Waterloo, England) Manor
Road, Walton-on-Thames, Surrey KT12 2PF

WHEN: Session 7 – 1:30 PM, Sunday, March 4, 2007

TICKETS:
◦ Box office: 01932 253354 (24 hours)
◦ Session 7 Only: £8, concessions £5, students with card £2 (includes
2:30 PM concert by Wim Hoogewerf performing works for microtonal
guitar)
◦ Conference Pass: £40, concessions £25, students £10

More information on UK Microfest 2 and its artistic director Donald
Bousted may be found at www.microtonalprojects.co.uk
2 LONDONMET

WHERE: London Metropolitan University 41-71 Commercial Road, London E1
1LA

WHEN: Monday, March 5, 2007

◦ This event is free and open to the public.

More information on Londonmet, and the coordinator of the festival
Lewis Jones may be found at www.londonmet.ac.uk.
This event is made possible in part by the UK Musical Acoustics
Network.

More information on H-Pi products and email contact may be found at
www.h-pi.com
