I'm now working on a new idea - a simultaneous toccata and fugue (more in the style of Prokofiev than Bach) in, what else, 72-edo. This is due to be my toughest project so far. In the meantime...

http://www.makamhane.com/nota_archive/index.php

This archive is in English, except for the music of course. It looks like it hasn't been updated in a while (the last update is dated 31 January 2007), and there are no sound files, but there is a search engine where you can search for makam (mode/scale), form, usul (rhythm/meter) or composer name.

There are 125 makamlar listed in the repertoire, but I haven't checked if there is sheet music for every one. They also give a shoutout to Lilypond (which, as we discussed here not too long ago, can now print Turkish accidentals), so they must use it to make some of the scores.

A lot of the music is available at other archives, most of them being in Turkish.
