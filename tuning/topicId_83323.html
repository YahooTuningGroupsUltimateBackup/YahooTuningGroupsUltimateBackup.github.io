In Latin "calcare" means "to kick", as in the proverb "Mors calcat ipso pede
... " (Death strikes with the same foot ...).
Thus in pre-electric times, when bellows were operated by a man with his
feet, he was called "the kicker", i.e. calcant.
The stop would activate a signal, e.g. draw from a chord sounding a small
bell in the blower's room, warning the "calcant" to start blowing as the
organist was about to start playing.
