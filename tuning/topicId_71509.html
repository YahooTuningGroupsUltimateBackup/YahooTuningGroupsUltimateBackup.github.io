Dear Serge,

I notice, that you have seen fit to dedicate only 13 pages to so
comprehensive a topic as what you deign to refer to as "Arabo-oriental
Music", as though the musical genres of the Near East had nothing to do with
anyone else other than Arabs?

And, by calling this "a big entire chapter", you surely exaggerate?

Candidly, I am a bit dissapointed. While the inclusion of Safi-al-Din
Urmavi, who was an Abbasid Muslim of Turkic lineage, is much appreciated,
the book seems to say (as I can glean from the summary) nothing much about
the rest of the world after the Renaissance, and thus, appears to be
Eurocentric. Are you sure the title is appropriate? Would you not rather
consider renaming it "A Concise History of General Musical Acoustics"?

Unfortunately, I do not read French. Still, if you think it useful, you can
send me the said chapter at ozanyarman@ozanyarman.com for future scrutiny.

Congratulations on your efforts.

Cordially,
Oz.
