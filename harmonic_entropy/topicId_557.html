The validation exercise checks that the sum of the unnormalized
"probabilities" is a constant across all cents values. When computing the
entropy function of the "probabilities" for a given cents value, I normalize
the "probabilities" (by dividing by the sum) so that they sum to one. The
validation exercise is meant to insure that this normalization process
ITSELF is not contributing anything significant to the shape of the curve.
In this case, I'm afraid I can't guarantee that.

>() Noticed that the explanation of what a Vos curve is never made
>it to this list.

Search for "Vos" on the tuning list. Basically, it's exp(-|d/t|), where d is
the deviation in cents and t is a constant (which, amazingly, seems to have
little or no effect on the shape of the curve!).
